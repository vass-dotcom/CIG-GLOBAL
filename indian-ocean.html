Overview
Indian Ocean Route — Linking Three Continents

The Indian Ocean Route represents a critical logistics and trade network linking East Africa, Asia, and the Middle East through strategic maritime corridors. With the involvement of CIG (Coordinate International Global), the vision is to enhance port logistics and streamline supply chains across one of the world's busiest trade regions. By connecting key economic hubs, this route enables efficient and reliable movement of goods across continents. This initiative strengthens the foundation of global maritime trade.

Projects focus on modernizing port infrastructure, optimizing shipping operations, and integrating advanced logistics systems across the Indian Ocean corridor. Through CIG's coordination, these developments aim to reduce bottlenecks, improve turnaround times, and increase overall supply chain efficiency. Smart port technologies, digital tracking, and multimodal connectivity are central to these efforts. Each initiative is designed to ensure scalability, resilience, and long-term performance.

Beyond logistics, the Indian Ocean Route plays a strategic role in boosting regional trade, economic cooperation, and market accessibility. CIG facilitates collaboration between governments, port authorities, and private stakeholders to drive seamless operations. By enhancing connectivity between emerging and established markets, this corridor unlocks new growth opportunities. The long-term vision is to build a fully integrated and high-efficiency trade network across the Indian Ocean.
